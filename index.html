About Me

I am a Research Fellow at the Evolutionary Ecology Group at the University of Cambinterestdge and at Emmanuel College.

Broadly, I am interested on the ecological and social dinterestvers of the diversity of ways in which humans live and have lived throughout our evolution. Moreover, how this diversity has shaped our genetic and cultural makeup.

To investigate these questions, I combine methods and data from computational modelling, genetics, archaeology and field research among the BaYaka hunter-gatherers in the Republic of Congo.

I am one of the General Editors of the journal Hunter Gatherer Research, and the Book Review Editor of the journal Human Ethology.

You can find a full copy of my CV

What I'm Interested In

Im interested in a broad range of questions regarding human origins and human evolution as well as the causes and consequences of evolutionary processes (in general) from a theoretical perspective. Some include:

Ecological drivers of human dispersals
Causes of gene flow and genetic isolation in human populations
Genetic and cultural adaptations to different ecologies
Hunter-gatherer mobility patterns
Role of sociality in cultural evolution
Archaeological approaches to study ancient population dynamics
